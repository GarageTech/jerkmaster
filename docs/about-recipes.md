# About the Recipes

The recipes included with JerkMaster were not copied from a dehydrator manual, a marketing brochure, or one of the countless generic drying tables found on the internet.

Many commercial dehydrators provide recipe books that consist of little more than a list of fruits, vegetables, mushrooms, fish and meat followed by a recommendation such as "dry for 8–12 hours". While these tables may be useful as a starting point, they rarely provide a repeatable process capable of producing consistent, high-quality jerky.

The recipes and drying profiles included with JerkMaster are based on a real-world jerky-making workflow that has been refined through practical use. They are not theoretical examples. They are recipes that are regularly used to produce jerky on a weekly basis and are expected to deliver the same predictable result every time.

The recipe base and preparation methodology used by this project were developed by the author of the Dobroslav13 channel:

[youtube.com/@dobroslav13](https://www.youtube.com/@dobroslav13)

Detailed videos describing the process can be found here:

- [Jerky preparation video 1](https://youtu.be/KUhFk8br9YQ)
- [Jerky preparation video 2](https://youtu.be/UZYT2PxXyAo)

One of the defining characteristics of these recipes is their flavor profile. Compared to many commercial jerky products, which are often relatively mild and neutral, these recipes tend to be bold, rich, spicy and highly aromatic. They were designed to produce a distinctive and memorable result rather than the lowest common denominator suitable for mass production.

Of course, taste is highly personal.

The recipes included with JerkMaster should be viewed as a proven starting point rather than strict rules. Users are encouraged to adapt them to their own preferences and intended use. Salt levels, pepper content, spice blends, sweetness and other ingredients can be adjusted to create anything from a mild everyday protein snack to a heavily seasoned product intended for outdoor activities, travel or pairing with beverages.

The goal of JerkMaster is not to dictate how jerky should taste. The goal is to provide a reliable and repeatable process so that once you discover a flavor profile you love, you can reproduce it consistently whenever you want.
